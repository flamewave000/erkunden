# Erkunden

## Spaceship

A spaceship is used by the player to travel across the galaxy. A player can own multiple ships but must either hire a pilot and crew, buy service droids, or build their own AI to run the other ships.

### Rooms

​    The entire ship can be customized but a given ship will only have so much space. Player buys a ship structure that can contain multiple levels and various gridded build space for expansion. Any space not being used is automatically set as basic storage area.  
​    Depending on the room type, a service droid or hired crew can run it autonomously. Any room that has no crew/droid must be run by the player. Various tasks can take time and have multiple steps which the player would need to manually perform. An automated room allows the player to queue up tasks that will be automatically produced. The amount of floor space in a room defines how many workers can operate in the room at the same time. Note that room equipment will take up floor space, and may reduce the available worker slots.  
​    The player can only operate in one room at a time, which is why it is necessary for them to hire a crew that can take on tasks for them. This also means they cannot be manually piloting the ship while they are performing other actions.

## Vehicles

Vehicles can be manufactured or purchased. They can be either land or air. They can be transported in a cargo hold, but will required manual deployment and assembly. If a ship has a Vehicle Bay large enough for the vehicle, a player can quickly deploy and collect the vehicle without having to assemble/disassembled it each time. Vehicles can also be repaired in a vehicle bay, otherwise they can only be repaired at a station with vehicle maintenance

Vehicles also run on rechargeable batteries. Vehicles will be automatically recharged in vehicle bays using the ship's power.

## Energy

All equipment, including the ship's engine require energy. This energy can be directly controlled for each device. Some equipment requires a specific amount of power, while others can operate sub-optimally with less power. For example, the engines can function at 50% power, but they will only provide 50% thrust. Other devices such as automated repair systems may take longer to complete their tasks when run with less power.

A player can also over-charge equipment for a short amount of time in order to boost the performance. For example, the engine thrusters can be overloaded for a short boost of thrust, but will take time to cool back down. If shields are being depleted, you can give the shield generator a boost to increase the shield regeneration for a short while.

These overrides will consume large amounts of the ship's power which will need to be regenerated from the ship's power plant.

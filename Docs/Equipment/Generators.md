# Generators

There are a couple types of generators. These are systems that draw heavily on a ship's power plant and capacitors to operate.

## Shield Generator

Creates a pulsing energy shield around the ship. The more energy put into the shield, the higher the percentage of damage reduction. When an impact occurs, a percentage of that impact is absorbed by the shield and dissipated as thermal radiation. Damage absorbed by an impact causes the shields to directly draw instantaneous power from the ship's capacitors.

If the capacitors do not have enough energy, the surplus damage reaches the ship. If the capacitor is depleted, the shield become completely inert in-spite of still being deployed and powered by the power plant.

Shields present a visual cue as to how low a ship's capacitor is, or how much energy is being channeled to the engines.

## Worm Hole Generator

A Worm Hole Generator is a system that requires a large amount of room space. These also consume a massive amount of energy from both directly from the Power Plant, and instantaneous power from the Capacitors. These generate an artificial Worm Hole to another point in space. This is the primary mode of transportation between [Astral Systems](../Environment/Astral System.md). While a Player can perform interstellar travel using an FTL Engine, those would take a long duration of time, whereas a Worm Hole provides near instant travel.

### WHG Stations

There are several active networks of Worm Hole Generator stations throughout the galaxy where players can pay to be sent to any system in the galaxy that is on the network. This often means players cannot jump into enemy territory as the networks they are friendly with would not have WHG Stations in those systems.

### Distance

Worm Holes are limited in their distance, and can only reach up to a maximum distance per jump. Players will need to perform "hops" to get to distant Astral Systems. This distance will depend on the amount of power a ship provides.

### Capacity

A Worm Hole can only handle a certain volume of travellers. For example, a Worm Hole large enough for only one [Battleship](../Ships/Battleship.md) could instead allow several smaller [Corvettes](../Ships/Corvette.md) through. In order to increase the size of the worm hole, larger capacitors are needed to tear open the hole wider. Then the larger the hole, the more continuous energy from the ship's Power Plant is needed to maintain the stability. The less energy put out, the less time the hole will stay open for.

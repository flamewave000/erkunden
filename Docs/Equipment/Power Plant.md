# Power Plants

These are a ship's primary energy source. They consume fuel to provide a continuous output of electrical energy. They harness Nuclear Fusion to produce Megawatts of energy from a very small amount of fuel. If a player were to run out of fuel, they can deploy solar sails that collect the solar radiation of the system and can charge their ship.

## Fuel

**Deuterium and Tritium** are the fuel sources of the fusion reactors. These provide long term, sustainable energy to power a ship. Fuel is most often cheaply obtained, but can be scarce in certain [Astral Systems](../Environment/Astral System.md). Mining this fuel provides stable income as the price does not fluctuate.

## Solar Sails

Large reinforced solar sensitive sheets fold out to the sides of a ship. These provide a minimal amount of power. This can be used to recharge capacitors, or even power an engine at minimal velocity. The more sails a ship has, the more energy it can collect for recharging, or powering systems.

## Plant Types

There are various sizes of power plant. Each one requires a minimal amount of room space on a ship to occupy. The larger the plant, the more energy it can produce, and the more fuel it consumes.

## Capacitors

Every power plant will have a capacitor built into it that can be charges over time from the powerplant or solar sails, and can be discharged for high-energy demands, such as firing powerful weapons, absorbing shield impacts. Ships can increase their capacitance by purchasing additional capacitor that take up Room Space on their ship.

> NOTE: Shields require a constant power output to establish and maintain, but impacts directly consume energy from the capacitor to reduce incoming attacks. Shields also do not 100% block attacks. Once the capacitor is depleted, the shields will cease to stop impacts.
